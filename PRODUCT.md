# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Greek-speaking content creators, video editors, podcasters, and social media producers who work with audio in Greek. They sit in an edit suite (or a home office that functions like one) generating subtitles for YouTube, TikTok, Reels, or broadcast-quality clips. They're technically comfortable but not developers. They care about accuracy, speed, and results — not about the tool itself.

## Product Purpose

GreekSRT converts audio (MP3, M4A, WAV) into grammar-aware Greek subtitle files. It uses AI transcription (ElevenLabs, Groq, OpenAI Whisper) and applies Greek linguistics rules so subtitle breaks never land at "και" or split an article from its noun. Users also get an inline editor, audio sync playback, text processing (censoring, number conversion, time formatting), animated subtitle export (ASS + WebM), and a full style configurator. The goal: eliminate every post-processing step a creator would normally do in Premiere, DaVinci, or CapCut. Success = SRT downloaded in under 3 minutes, zero manual cleanup.

## Brand Personality

Precise, focused, professional. Like a tool made by someone who uses it. Not flashy. Earns trust through capability, not decoration.

Three words: **focused · precise · earned**

## Anti-references

- Generic online converter sites — ad-heavy, cluttered, untrustworthy
- AI-generated purple-gradient dark UIs — glassmorphism, glowing card borders on everything, rainbow accents
- Heavy enterprise dashboards — navy + data table corporate feel

Reference feel: Runway ML, Descript, Opus Clip — dark, confident, purposeful studio tools that disappear into the task.

## Design Principles

1. **The tool disappears.** UI exists to serve the workflow, not to be noticed. Every element earns its presence by reducing friction.
2. **State is legible.** Transcribing, editing, playing, error — each state is visually unambiguous at a glance.
3. **Density is earned.** Compact where users are scanning (subtitle list), spacious where they're deciding (settings, export).
4. **Confidence through restraint.** A muted palette with one precise accent reads as competent. Decoration reads as insecurity.
5. **Greek is first-class.** The product is Greek-native. Typography, spacing, and label copy should respect that.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast. Focus states visible on all interactive elements (critical for keyboard-only subtitle editing). Reduced-motion alternative for all transitions. Greek and English labels both readable at any zoom level.
